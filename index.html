conventional-commit-helper

A shell script to assist with conventional commits

If you've worked with me personally you probably already know I am a big fan of good practices in development. I do what I can to create consistency and predictability. Throughout 2020 I've grown increasingly fond of Conventional Commits since stumbling across them in Facebook's Open Source project Docusaurus.

Now I'm not here to convince anyone to do anything so I will let you decide for yourself if conventional commits are right for you. All I will say is that they do come in handy, but even tasks as trivial as commits can become flawed. Here's some issue’s I've ran into throughout my experience with Conventional Commits across teams:

  • Syntax can be tedious to remember
  • Someone forgets or neglects the commit type

and this is exactly what I've aimed to fix with the conventional-commit-helper. Assisting with the standard that comes with Conventional Commits, conventional-commit-helper is for consistency. The default shell command is established for micro commits, establish the alias and your on the road. For the full setup guide, head over to the docs below.

lg-coffee

Bootstrap a serverless e-commerce app and easily manage your products and orders via the Stripe Dashboard.

Ever have a product or service you think you could sell, but just can’t justify hosting fees for a website at the current point in time? Say for example, an artist marketplace where product isn’t expected to sell daily. With lg-coffee your products exist in their own domain completely controlled by you.

Ready to scale?

lg-coffee was designed with scalability in mind from day 1. The entire storefront is configured via an JavaScript Object, just add a backend of your choosing.

Why should I trust lg-coffee?

Understandably, you should be wary of anything that accepts payment. While I'd encourage you to get familiar with the ins and outs of lg-coffee, here's a few reasons to feel at ease:

  • Stripe manages all payment intents, product information and order details.
  • Storefront is built using Stripe Elements and Next.js. Source code includes type checking, strict test cases and has been enforced using the Airbnb style guide in collaboration with ESLint and Prettier.
  • Payment intents happen on the backend.
  • lg-coffee does not collect information on anyone, ever.
